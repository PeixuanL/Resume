## 0. 这一轮面试的真实目标

外籍 HR 一面不是深挖产品方案，而是判断你是否值得进入后续业务面：

1. 英文沟通是否自然，能否进行产品讨论。
2. 动机是否合理，不像“随便从 B 端转 C 端”。
3. 简历里的产品全生命周期、0-1、AI / Agent、跨团队协作是否真实。
4. 你是否理解 Trucker Path 不是普通社区产品，而是位置数据、实时信息、司机工作流和社区贡献结合的垂直行业 App。
5. 对薪资、通勤、到岗时间、行业学习曲线是否可控。

一句话策略：

> Do not sell yourself as a senior consumer social PM. Sell yourself as a product manager who can turn complex real-world workflows into structured product solutions, and who can transfer that ability to location-based, real-time, community-powered products.

中文意思：

> 不要把自己包装成资深 C 端社区 PM。你要把自己定位为“复杂真实场景产品经理”，能把这套能力迁移到位置数据、实时信息、司机工作流和 UGC 信息质量。

## 2. 你的主叙事

### 中文定稿记录

```text
您好，非常感谢您今天抽时间和我沟通。我是李沛宣，目前在 DataMesh 做 B 端产品经理。虽然我之前负责的产品和 Trucker Path 这种 C 端 App 不完全一样，但我过去有几项能力，我认为是可以迁移的。

第一，是复杂场景拆解和产品方案输出的能力。我之前负责的产品更多面向工业和楼宇运维场景，业务流程比较复杂。以设施运维产品为例，我会先梳理一线用户从发现问题到处理问题的完整路径，再把这个路径拆成告警、设备信息、资产管理和工单流转等核心模块，最后形成研发和设计可以执行的 MVP 方案。

第二，是跨团队协作和价值传递的能力。在 0 到 1 产品推进中，我经常和研发、设计、测试团队配合，把模糊需求圈定边界，拆成 MVP 或 POC 阶段可以落地的范围，并明确优先级和核心用户路径。产品初步成型后，我也会支持演示视频、宣传素材和产品说明内容的制作，向售前和项目团队传递产品价值，帮助他们理解产品解决什么问题、适合什么场景。

第三，是快速学习和跨领域迁移的能力。我在接手设施运维方向时，需要在较短时间内补充楼宇运维、IDC 机房、设备告警和工单流程相关知识，并在一个月左右完成第一阶段产品规划，随后和研发团队推进 Demo 输出。所以我比较习惯快速进入新领域，并把学习结果转成具体方案和交付物。

所以虽然我不是直接做过 Trucker Path 这类 C 端社区和导航产品，但我理解它不是泛娱乐社区，而是围绕卡车司机真实工作流、位置数据、实时信息和用户贡献来解决实际问题的产品。我希望把自己在复杂场景拆解、跨团队落地和价值传递、快速学习上的经验，迁移到一个更贴近用户、反馈更快、也更强调真实场景理解的产品里。
```

## 4. Top 5 必练题：英文 STAR / CAR 模板

### Q1. Tell me about yourself.

能力标签：Communication / Positioning
框架：短版 CAR，不要讲成长史。

```text
Thank you for this opportunity. I’m glad to speak with you today.

My name is Peixuan Li, and I’m currently a B2B product manager at DataMesh, focusing on digital twin and facility operations products.

My main strength is breaking down complex real-world workflows and turning them into clear product solutions. For example, in a facility operations product, I mapped the user journey from issue discovery to resolution, and turned it into modules such as asset management and work orders for the MVP.

In this process, I worked closely with engineering, design, and testing teams to define the product scope and move the demo forward. I also supported demo videos, product materials, and internal explanations, so sales and project teams could communicate the product value clearly.

Since facility operations was also a new domain for me, I had to quickly understand building operations, data centers, and related operational workflows, and translate that understanding into a clear product plan and demo scope within about one month.

Although I have not directly worked on trucking, navigation, or consumer community products before, I understand Trucker Path as a practical product that helps drivers make better decisions through real-time information and user contributions. I hope to bring my structured problem-solving, fast learning, and cross-functional execution experience to this role.
```

中文记忆点：

- 当前身份：DataMesh B端产品经理。
- 核心能力：复杂真实工作流 → 结构化产品方案。
- 事实证据：11 个版本、PRD/原型、跨团队、交付验证。
- 转向逻辑：不是想“转 C 端”，而是想做“更大用户规模 + 全球产品 + 实时信息/位置数据”。

### Q2. Your background is more B2B. Why this consumer-facing role?

能力标签：Self-awareness / Learning / Role Fit
框架：承认差异 → 建立连接 → 表达成长。

```text
That’s a fair question. My background is indeed more B2B and industrial, so I would not position myself as a traditional consumer social product manager.

But I don’t see this as a completely unrelated switch. In my current work, I also need to understand user scenarios, break down workflows, define product requirements, work with design and engineering, and validate whether the product actually solves the user’s problem.

For Trucker Path, the users and business context are different, but the product logic is connected: drivers need reliable real-time information, better route planning, parking availability, fuel prices, weigh station status, and community updates. These are practical workflow problems.

So I would bring structured product thinking and cross-functional delivery experience, while ramping up quickly on consumer metrics, UGC mechanisms, and driver community engagement.
```

不要说：

- I want to switch to C-side product.
- I think consumer products are easier.
- I know trucking very well.

### Q3. Can you walk me through your 0-to-1 product experience?

能力标签：Ownership / Dealing with Ambiguity / Product Lifecycle
故事：AI 设施运维产品（0→1）
框架：STAR。

```text
One representative 0-to-1 experience is an AI-enabled facility operations product I worked on at DataMesh.

The situation was that many facility operation scenarios were highly customized. Equipment information, alarms, work orders, SOPs, and operational knowledge were scattered across different systems, so delivery was difficult to standardize.

My task was to help define a more reusable product workflow. On the product side, I worked on requirement analysis, product scope, PRD and prototype logic, and cross-functional alignment with engineering, customer success, and delivery teams.

The core workflow we designed connected asset information, alarm management, work order flow, knowledge base, and AI-assisted diagnosis. I also helped define human confirmation and fallback rules, because in operational scenarios, AI output needs to be explainable and controllable.

The MVP covered most high-priority requirements from a benchmark client and supported several business opportunities. It also helped shorten delivery cycles in similar projects by moving repeated customer needs into a more standard product baseline.
```

你需要面前补的真实细节：

- 最能体现“你本人动作”的 1 个 PRD / 评审 / 取舍例子。
- “MVP 覆盖 80%+ 高优先级诉求”的边界怎么说。
- “5 家潜在商机”和“5–6 月到 1–2 月”的口述边界。

### Q4. Can you explain the AI / Agent product on your resume?

能力标签：Product Clarity / Technical Communication
框架：解释产品机制，不讲模型训练。

```text
In my resume, AI Agent and RAG refer to product mechanisms inside an operations workflow, not model training.

The idea is not just to add a chatbot. The product needs to help users understand an alarm, retrieve relevant equipment information, SOP knowledge, or historical work orders, suggest possible causes, and then help generate a next-step action or work order draft.

My focus was on the product side: defining the workflow, input and output boundaries, what information the AI should use, when human confirmation is required, and what fallback rules are needed when confidence is low.

The reason this matters is that in operational products, users need trustworthy and explainable decision support. I think there is a similar product principle in Trucker Path: drivers also need timely and reliable information to make better decisions on the road.
```

一句边界句，必须会说：

```text
I focused on product design, workflow, input-output boundaries, human confirmation, and fallback rules, not model training.
```

### Q5. Why Trucker Path / Moatable?

能力标签：Motivation / Company Fit
框架：官网事实 → 产品兴趣 → 你的连接。

```text
From my understanding, Moatable builds and scales AI-native SaaS products, and Trucker Path is one of its portfolio companies.

What attracts me about Trucker Path is that it is not only a navigation app. It supports truck drivers and fleets with truck-safe navigation, trip planning, parking information, fuel prices, weigh station status, load-related services, and driver community.

I like products where user value is concrete. For truck drivers, accurate and timely information can directly affect time, cost, safety, and comfort on the road.

This connects with my own experience because I have worked on products where real-time information, workflow efficiency, and trustworthy decision support are important. I’m interested in applying that product thinking to a larger, consumer-facing, global product.
```

如果 HR 问“你了解 Moatable 吗”，不要展开太多公司金融背景，回答到这里即可。

## 6. 缺口清单和救场句

### 缺口 1：没有直接 trucking 经验

不要硬说懂。这样说：

```text
I don’t want to overclaim direct trucking industry experience. What I can bring is a structured way to learn the domain: user interviews, workflow mapping, competitor analysis, and identifying the key moments where information quality affects user decisions.
```

### 缺口 2：没有典型 C 端社区 PM 背景

```text
I’m still growing in direct consumer community experience. But I understand that for a product like Trucker Path, community is not just social interaction. It is also a way to collect fresh, trustworthy, location-based information from users. That connection is very interesting to me.
```

### 缺口 3：增长指标不强

```text
I would focus on learning and tracking metrics such as activation, retention, contribution rate, content freshness, and the percentage of users who trust or act on community updates.
```

### 缺口 4：App 经验可能被质疑

```text
My previous work was not a pure consumer mobile app, but I have worked on user journeys, product flows, prototype logic, and front-end interaction requirements. For this role, I would pay more attention to mobile onboarding, repeated usage, and daily engagement.
```
